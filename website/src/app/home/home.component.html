Hope Springs Christian Learning Center serves children with learning differences in a distinctly Christian setting. We empower children to fulfill God's purpose for their lives by developing their unique abilities.

Hope Springs serves children in grades 3 through 8. We serve children who have Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), a mild level of Autism, Dyscalculia, Dysgraphia, Dyslexia, and/or other specific learning disabilities. Many students who come to our school have had an Individual Education Plan (IEP) in public school. We are not a good fit for children who are diagnosed with a moderate to severe intellectual disability, severe autism, or behavioral difficulties.

NILD Educational Therapy is the Foundation of our Teaching

Hope Springs is one of a few schools in the Atlanta area that uses the National Institute for Learning Development (NILD) Educational Therapy®. We are the only school that offers NILD Education Therapy as part of our daily curriculum.

This therapy helps students develop tools for independent learning in the classroom and in life. Our NILD program is based on Orton-Gillingham research, with more comprehensive application by current brain research. It is also approved by the International Dyslexia Association. NILD Educational Therapy® focuses on four key components: cognition, perception, academics and emotions.

This therapy strengthens our students' education and helps them gain confidence to progress toward standard curriculum environments offered in traditional school environments. Learn more about our approach.
